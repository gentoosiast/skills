# SPEAKER-PROFILE.md format

A living record of how *this* speaker sounds — used to keep every talk in their voice instead of a generic conference register. The talk-prep analogue of the notes a ghostwriter keeps on the person they write for.

Lives at `talk-settings/SPEAKER-PROFILE.md`, one per working directory. Formed up front by the speaker interview on a first run (see [SKILL.md](./SKILL.md)); refined over time as the speaker corrects how they sound.

## Structure

```md
# Speaker profile

## Speaker & angle
- <who they are to the room; first-person-from-experience vs impersonal; how they enter a topic> <!-- YYYY-MM-DD -->

## Audience
- <who's in the seats, their level, the base NOT to re-explain; place in a series> <!-- YYYY-MM-DD -->

## Register & tone
- <form of address: direct/impersonal; in languages that mark it, which form — held, never mixed> <!-- YYYY-MM-DD -->
- <conversational/formal; humor allowed and at whose expense; what reads as lecturing-down> <!-- YYYY-MM-DD -->

## Avoid (stop-list)
- <moves this speaker won't ship: filler, clickbait, jargon-for-jargon, invented numbers, one-true-way selling> <!-- YYYY-MM-DD -->

## Fact-check bar
- <how hard to verify claims; how uncertainty is marked> <!-- YYYY-MM-DD -->

## Naming
- <honest hook vs clickbait; the keywords that matter> <!-- YYYY-MM-DD -->
```

## Rules

- **Form it before drafting, on a first run.** No profile means run the interview and write this file first — don't infer a voice silently and don't start the outline until it's down.
- **One trait per bullet** — concrete, dated with a `<!-- YYYY-MM-DD -->` comment.
- **Append, don't overwrite.** A changed preference gets a new dated bullet; the history shows how the voice shifted and guards against drifting back.
- **Refine only on a real correction.** When the speaker says "I wouldn't put it that way," capture the *rule* behind it, not just the single fix.
- **Voice only — no talk content.** What a given talk is about lives in its `CONTEXT.md`, never here. The profile holds only how this speaker sounds.
- **Short enough to read at the top of every run.** A profile too long to skim stops getting held.
